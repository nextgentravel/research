Call for User Research Participants

Public Services and Procurement Canada - NextGen Travel Program, are looking for employees in the federal government to discuss their travel experience in the federal public service.

Continuing to build on our cross-country engagement tour with hundreds of public servants like yourself, we've partnered with Code for Canada to imagine what a user-centric business travel service could look like.

We are looking to gather more feedback, in the form of one-on-one interviews, surveys and other research. Interested? Please fill in the form below and we'll be in touch!

Code for Canada is a national nonprofit that connects government innovators with the tech and design community. Our programs enable governments to deliver better digital public services and empower communities to solve civic challenges using technology and design.

Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) - NextGen Travel Program is committed to protecting the privacy rights of individuals.

The definition of “personal information” is any information, in any form, about an identifiable individual. Refer to section 3 of the Privacy Act for further details regarding personal information.

The personal information you provide will be used by Public Services and Procurement Canada for research and development purposes; and is collected under the authority of section 11 of Public Service Employment Act. Provision of your information is voluntary, and will not be used to make any administrative decisions about you.

Personal information is collected and used as described in the Treasury Board Secretariat's Standard Personal Information Bank Outreach Activities (PSU 938)

Individuals have the right to file a complaint with the Privacy Commissioner of Canada regarding the department's handling of their personal information.



Research Participant Diversity & Inclusivity

Inclusivity and accessibility are very important to us. As part of our research, we aim to collect information on, and speak to, a pool of participants that is diverse and representative of the people who work within the federal public service. This information will be kept confidential and will only be used to ensure equitable participation.
